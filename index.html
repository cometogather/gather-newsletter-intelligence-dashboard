Key Insights & Observations

Open Rates

All 6 newsletters exceed the 21.5% GA benchmark in every issue. FinTech Pulse leads at 44.5% in March — driven by timely Fed rate commentary and CFO-specific subject lines.

Content Mix

Data & Research Roundup formats drive the highest clicks (9.2%), followed by Case Studies (8.1%). Opinion pieces underperform at 3.8% — consider repositioning as SME-attributed commentary.

SME Effect

Named SME content lifts CTOR by 22% on average. Q1 saw 14 active contributors across 6 clients. Supply Chain Lens issues 2–3 show recovery correlated with Aisha Farooq's added contributions.

List Health

Unsubscribe rates are well below the 0.5% concern threshold across all newsletters. Supply Chain Lens Iss.1 (0.22%) was the highest — addressed by improving topic relevance in Iss.2 and 3.

Pipeline

79 new opportunities tracked to newsletter engagement in Q1, with FinTech Pulse contributing the highest estimated pipeline value ($1.82M). Meeting/demo bookings account for 36% of CRM events.

Growth

Total subscriber base grew 9% vs Q4 2025, reaching 47,200. Tech Horizons added the most net subscribers (+320), driven by an organic referral campaign in February.

Q2 2026 Recommendations

01

Expand SME programme across all newsletters. Prioritise Supply Chain Lens and ESG Briefing — both show CTOR uplift when SME content is present, but have the lowest contributor frequency in Q1. Target 2 SME appearances per issue.

02

Shift Supply Chain Lens to fortnightly send cadence. Three monthly issues aren't sufficient to maintain top-of-mind with a logistics audience navigating real-time tariff and freight-rate volatility. Test biweekly in Q2.

03

Introduce Data & Research Roundup format to ESG Briefing. Currently ESG Briefing leans on regulatory summaries; adding a proprietary data section (e.g. ESG scoring trends, disclosure rates by sector) should lift CTOR toward the programme average.

04

A/B test numbered-list subject lines across all newsletters. Q1 analysis shows subject lines with specific numbers ("3 tools", "5 things", "4 pivots") outperform non-numbered equivalents by ~18% open rate. Formalise as a standing test.

05

Build a re-engagement sequence for non-openers (>90 days). Current list hygiene is strong, but a structured win-back series (2 emails, 14-day gap) could recover 3–5% of dormant subscribers before Q3 list scrub.

06

Deepen CRM attribution to multi-touch. Current last-touch attribution undervalues newsletters that initiate the buyer journey. Work with client HubSpot admins to enable multi-touch attribution reporting in Q2.
